Lemon Ricotta Pancakes

Just listening to the name of this recipe makes me want to salivate PERGHHHH!

I know it's supposed to be a breakfast recipe but I think this would be great even at night. It's not difficult to make either.

If these pancakes were any lighter, they would float off the plate!

Here's what you need:

  • 3/4 cup cold water or milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 tablespoon grated lemon zest (just the yellow part of the skin)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/8 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 cup self-rising flour
  • 2 tablespoons self-rising flour

Directions:

  1. Whisk water and baking soda together in a mixing bowl. Add ricotta cheese, lemon zest, vegetable oil, sugar, egg and vanilla. Whisk until smooth, breaking up lumps of cheese as you mix. Add melted butter, lemon juice, and 1 cup plus 2 tablespoons self-rising flour. Whisk together, stirring until most of flour disappears into the batter
  2. Let the batter sit at room temperature about 15 minutes.
  3. Heat lightly greased cast iron skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Portion out 1/4 cup scoops of batter onto skillet (cook in batches). When bubbles begin to form on the surface and the edges of the pancakes look dry, (2 to 3 minutes), flip and cook other side until cooked through, 2 to 3 minutes. Transfer to a warm plate.

Serves 2 large or 4 small servings. ENJOY!
